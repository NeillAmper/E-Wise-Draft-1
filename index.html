      What is E-Waste?

      Electronic waste, or e-waste, describes discarded electrical or electronic devices. Used electronics which are destined for refurbishment, reuse, resale, salvage recycling through material recovery, or disposal are also considered e-waste. It is one of the fastest-growing waste streams in the world.

      The Dangers of Improper Disposal

      E-waste contains toxic materials such as lead, mercury, cadmium, and beryllium. Improperly disposing of these items can cause these chemicals to leach into our soil and water, posing significant risks to human health and the environment.
